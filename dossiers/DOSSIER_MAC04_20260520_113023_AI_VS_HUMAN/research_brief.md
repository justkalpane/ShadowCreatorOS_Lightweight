# Research Brief: AI vs Human

## Research objective
Build a script that moves beyond cliches by showing where AI clearly outperforms humans, where humans remain structurally better, and where collaboration is the realistic competitive model.

## Core comparative frame

1. AI strength category: speed, scale, pattern extraction, consistency.
2. Human strength category: judgment under ambiguity, ethical accountability, lived context, emotional resonance.
3. Conflict category: deskilling risk, over-trust in confident outputs, speed pressure vs truth quality.
4. Synthesis category: hybrid workflows where AI drafts and humans decide.

## Useful tension points for script

- AI can process ten thousand comments in minutes; humans can detect when the room is afraid but pretending not to be.
- AI can generate options fast; humans decide which option should not exist.
- AI can imitate style; humans can carry moral responsibility.
- The market rewards speed first, but trust compounds slower and lasts longer.

## Audience assumptions

- Creator/knowledge audience.
- High concern about job displacement.
- Curiosity about practical adaptation, not philosophical abstraction only.

## Script angle

Main thesis:
The real battle is not AI vs human intelligence in isolation. It is unmanaged automation vs human-led systems that use AI as leverage.

Secondary thesis:
People who treat AI as a junior collaborator will outperform people who treat AI as an autonomous replacement.

## Evidence design notes (non-API, repo-first)

- No provider calls or external execution claims.
- Use grounded examples and operational logic rather than fabricated stats.
- Keep examples concrete: hiring, education, customer support, creator workflows.
