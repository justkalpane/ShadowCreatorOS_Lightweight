# Debate

## Position A: Automate Everything Fast

- Advantage: immediate speed, lower manual effort, more output volume.
- Claim: market rewards velocity; strategy can be refined later.
- Risk blind spot: if assumptions are wrong, scale increases damage.

## Position B: Strategy-First, Then Automation

- Advantage: automation maps to defined goals, audience, and quality gates.
- Claim: strategic clarity compounds with AI execution speed.
- Risk blind spot: slower initial setup if teams over-plan.

## Contradiction Check

- A says volume creates learning.
- B says unguided volume creates misleading signals.
- Resolution: volume is useful only after objective and evaluation logic are explicit.

## Synthesis (Krishna-style framing)

- Use automation aggressively, but never unconstrained.
- Human leadership owns purpose, trade-offs, and governance.
- AI owns repetition, pattern acceleration, and throughput.
- Winning architecture: human strategy layer on top, AI execution layer below.

## Winner

Position B with synthesis.  
Reason: it preserves speed benefits while preventing strategy drift and quality collapse.
