# Final Script (YouTube Shorts, 60s)

## Delivery Script

Stop blaming AI tools.  
Most automation fails because humans skip strategy.

I see this everywhere:  
people build 20 workflows before answering 3 questions:
Who are we serving?  
What outcome matters?  
What does quality look like?

So they get this result:  
100 automated outputs... and zero real traction.

Why?  
Because bad workflows amplify bad decisions.  
Automation scales direction.  
If direction is wrong, failure scales faster.

AI is incredible at speed, pattern recognition, and repetition.  
But it does not own your priorities, your trade-offs, or your brand judgment.  
That is human work.

Here is the winning model:  
Human strategy sets goals, constraints, and quality gates.  
AI executes at scale inside that system.

One line to remember:  
Do not automate your confusion.  
Clarify first. Then multiply.
