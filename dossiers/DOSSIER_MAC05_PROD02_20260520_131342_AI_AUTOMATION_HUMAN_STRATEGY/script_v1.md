# Script V1 (YouTube Shorts, 60s)

If your AI automation keeps failing, it is probably not a tool problem.  
It is a strategy problem.

Most people automate the wrong order:  
tool first, thinking later.

So what happens?  
You get faster output, but weaker outcomes.  
More content, less relevance.  
More workflows, less direction.

Here is the truth: bad workflows do not fix bad decisions.  
They amplify them.

AI can draft, summarize, generate, and scale.  
But AI cannot decide your real priority trade-offs for you.  
It cannot own your positioning.  
It cannot protect your standards unless you define them first.

Human strategy should set:
- the goal
- the constraints
- the quality bar
- the stop conditions

Then AI becomes a multiplier.  
Without that, automation becomes expensive noise.

Build strategy first. Automate second.  
That is how AI systems win in real business.
