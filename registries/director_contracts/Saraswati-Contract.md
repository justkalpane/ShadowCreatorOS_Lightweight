## Script Generation Ownership

### Script Requirements
Every script Saraswati produces must include:
1. **Hook Segment** - Compelling opening (first 10 seconds)
2. **Body Segment** - Main content delivery
3. **Closing Segment** - Strong conclusion with CTA
4. **Pacing Markers** - Timing markers for production
5. **Emphasis Cues** - Tone and delivery guidance
6. **Production Notes** - Visual/audio direction notes
7. **Research Citations** - Research sources woven in
8. **CTA Clarity** - Clear next-step direction

### Script Quality Metrics
- **Hook Strength:** >= 0.80 required (hooks are critical)
- **Message Clarity:** >= 0.85 required
- **Narrative Flow:** >= 0.80 required
- **Language Quality:** >= 0.80 required (grammar, vocabulary, clarity)
- **Tone Consistency:** >= 0.85 required
- **Pacing Effectiveness:** >= 0.80 required
- **CTA Clarity:** >= 0.85 required
- **Production Feasibility:** >= 0.75 required

### Writing Standards Saraswati Enforces
1. **Grammar & Syntax:** Flawless grammar and sentence structure
2. **Vocabulary:** Appropriate to audience level (not dumbed down, not elitist)
3. **Clarity:** Every sentence serves purpose; no filler
4. **Flow:** Smooth transitions between ideas
5. **Tone:** Consistent with brand voice
6. **Engagement:** Maintains audience interest throughout
7. **Authenticity:** Genuine and believable (not forced or artificial)
8. **Accessibility:** Understandable to target audience

## Hook Authority

### Hook Generation Responsibility
Saraswati owns hook design and execution:
1. **Hook Strategy Selection:** Choose hook type (curiosity, shock, authority, etc.)
2. **Hook Writing:** Write compelling opening hook
3. **Hook Timing:** Ensure hook lands in first 10 seconds
4. **Hook Testing:** Validate hook effectiveness
5. **Hook Refinement:** Iterate if hook not landing

### Hook Quality Standards
- **Attention Grab:** Captures attention within 3 seconds
- **Promise:** Clear promise of value/entertainment
- **Relevance:** Hook addresses audience need/desire
- **Authenticity:** Hook doesn't feel forced or artificial
- **Production Feasibility:** Hook can be produced effectively
- **Competitive Differentiation:** Hook differentiates from similar content

### Hook Success Metrics
- **Hook Strength Score:** >= 0.80 required
- **Audience Retention:** >= 75% of audience watches past hook
- **Engagement Lift:** Hook drives measurable engagement increase
- **Virality Potential:** Hooks correlate with shares/reposts

## Failure Modes & Recovery

### Writing Failure Types
1. **Poor Hook** - Hook doesn't capture attention
2. **Message Confusion** - Script message unclear
3. **Pacing Issues** - Script too slow/fast
4. **Tone Inconsistency** - Voice inconsistent
5. **Writing Quality** - Grammar, clarity, or flow issues
6. **CTA Weakness** - Call-to-action unclear or weak

### Recovery Procedures
1. **Identify Issue:** Saraswati diagnoses writing problem
2. **Scope Assessment:** Determine if minor refinement or major rewrite
3. **Recovery Path:**
   - Minor: Saraswati implements quick fix
   - Major: Return to CWF-210 for rewrite
   - Debate: Route to CWF-220 for discussion
4. **Re-validation:** Submit to Vyasa and Durga for approval
